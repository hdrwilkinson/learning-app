# Our Solution

**How Cognia addresses the fundamental problems with learning.**

---

## The Core Idea

Cognia inverts the traditional learning model:

| Traditional | Cognia |
|-------------|--------|
| You adapt to the course | The course adapts to you |
| Content presented once | Content revisited at optimal intervals |
| Completion = success | Retention = success |
| Passive consumption | Active engagement required |
| Fixed difficulty | Progressive challenge |

The platform treats learning as a *system* with feedback loops, not a one-way pipeline.

## Built on Learning Science

Before diving into features, it's important to understand **why** Cognia works. The system is built on four evidence-based principles from cognitive science—the most robust findings we have about how human memory actually works.

| Principle | What It Means | How Cognia Applies It |
|-----------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Spaced Repetition** | Review at increasing intervals, not all at once | Algorithm schedules optimal review times |
| **Retrieval Practice** | Test yourself, don't just re-read | Quiz Mode is the core mechanic, not optional |
| **Interleaving** | Mix topics during practice | Daily queue mixes items from different lessons |
| **Elaboration** | Connect new info to what you know | AI tutoring asks why, builds connections |

These principles work together in every study session:

## Key Innovation: Information Points

The atomic unit of Cognia is the **Information Point (IP)**—a single, testable piece of knowledge.

Unlike traditional courses that chunk content into "lessons" or "chapters," Cognia breaks everything down to its smallest learnable components:

```
Traditional Course Structure:
├── Chapter 1: Introduction to Python
│   └── (30 minutes of mixed content)

Cognia Structure:
├── Lesson: Variables and Types
│   ├── IP: What is a variable?
│   ├── IP: Integer type
│   ├── IP: String type
│   ├── IP: Type conversion
│   └── IP: Variable naming rules
```

Why this matters:
- **Precise tracking**: We know exactly what you know vs. don't know
- **Targeted retrieval**: Quiz only what needs reinforcement (Retrieval Practice)
- **Clear dependencies**: Each IP declares what must come before it
- **Measurable progress**: Mastery is calculated per-IP, not per-chapter

## Key Innovation: Dual-Track Mastery

Cognia tracks two measures for every Information Point:

### 1. Current Mastery (Volatile)
- Changes rapidly with each quiz response ← *Retrieval Practice updates this*
- Reflects your *current* ability to recall
- Goes up with correct answers, down with incorrect

### 2. Baseline Mastery (Stable)
- Moves slowly over time
- Represents your *underlying* understanding
- Buffers against temporary slips

**Why two tracks?**

Single-measure systems are fragile. Have a bad day? One wrong answer tanks your "mastery" even if you know the concept well.

The dual-track system means:
- Temporary mistakes don't erase proven knowledge
- Long-term understanding is recognized
- Recovery is faster for concepts you've truly learned

## Key Innovation: Prescribed Study

Cognia tells you **what to study, when to study it**.

No decision fatigue. No "what should I review today?" The system calculates:
- Which IPs are due for review ← *Spaced Repetition*
- What difficulty of questions to ask ← *Desirable Difficulty*
- How many new items to introduce ← *Pacing*
- How to mix items from different lessons ← *Interleaving*

Your daily session is:
1. Open app
2. See your queue
3. Study
4. Done

The algorithm handles optimization. You handle engagement.
